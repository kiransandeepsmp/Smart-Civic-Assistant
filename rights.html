Right to Equality (Articles 14–18)

Ensures equal treatment under the law and prohibits discrimination based on religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth. It also abolishes untouchability and titles (except for military or academic distinctions).

Right to Freedom (Articles 19–22)

Guarantees fundamental freedoms such as speech, assembly, movement, association, residence, and profession. It also ensures personal liberty and legal protection during arrests or detentions.

Right against Exploitation (Articles 23–24)

Protects individuals from human trafficking, forced labor, and child labor in hazardous jobs, safeguarding the dignity of every citizen.

Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25–28)

Grants individuals and groups the freedom to practice, profess, and propagate any religion, and manage religious affairs without state interference.

Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29–30)

Preserves the cultural identity of minorities by allowing them to maintain their language and culture, and establish and run educational institutions.

Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

Empowers citizens to move the Supreme Court or High Courts to protect their fundamental rights through writs and legal actions.
