🧭 How Government Works

India is a sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic with a parliamentary system of government. It consists of three branches:

  • Legislative: The Parliament (Lok Sabha & Rajya Sabha) makes laws and represents the voice of the people.
  • Executive: Led by the Prime Minister, the executive branch implements laws through ministries and departments.
  • Judiciary: Independent from the other two, it ensures justice and checks the constitutionality of laws.

📘 Civic Education

Civic education teaches citizens about their rights, responsibilities, and the workings of government. It encourages democratic participation, informs voting decisions, and fosters public accountability.

  • Understanding the Constitution: Basic principles, including liberty, equality, and fraternity.
  • Legal Literacy: Knowing your rights under laws such as the RTI Act, Indian Penal Code, etc.
  • Democratic Values: Promotes tolerance, pluralism, and public dialogue.

📢 Advocacy & Public Voice

Advocacy refers to actions taken to influence public policies and bring social change. It includes community organizing, legal action, campaigning, and engaging with lawmakers.

  • Forms of Advocacy: Public protests, media engagement, petitions, lobbying, and social media campaigns.
  • Purpose: To ensure marginalized voices are heard and that government policies are fair and inclusive.
  • Tools: RTI requests, Public Grievance Portal, Social audits, Citizen charters.
